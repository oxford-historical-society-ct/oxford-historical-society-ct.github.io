Note from Dorothy A. DeBisschop

I am municipal historian in Oxford, Connecticut, and 2nd vice president of the Oxford Historical Society. We have recently experienced an unpleasant episode in town with an individual attempting to falsify the history of a long-closed private school, the Oxford Institute. The individual has put up a web site (oxford.org), where he makes a number of false claims. Our local Land Trust has officially voted to refuse the proposal he presents there.

          He has also made a false comment on the Historical Society's Facebook page, claiming that the Oxford Historical Society “preserve(s) the history of the prestigious Oxford Institute!” The school was a business and educational failure. It was short-lived, as can be demonstrated by the number of news clippings and dates listed below.

          I am especially annoyed that an individual would presume to use our local historical society, run by a group of dedicated and honest volunteers, to support what I consider a fraud to give some proposed school a long history that is false.

          The purpose of this listing is not to disparage the Oxford man who appears to have worked hard to get an education and tried unsuccessfully to establish a local school. These items from contemporary news sources demonstrate that claims made on the webpage oxford.org are not true. You are invited to compare the claims made in oxford.org to the news items from local newspapers. It will show a number of false statements there.

1893_08-03_SEYMOUR RECORD: Walter H.
