1882_12-15_Seymour-Record

 

 

OXFORD.

 

Henry A. Lane has moved into the house with Orrin Tucker.

 

Benjamin Nichols is moving to Ansonia.

 

The donation visit to Rev. Mr. Bailey last Tuesday evening was attended with satisfactory results.

 

There will be a Christmas-tree entertainment in St. Peter's church on Wednesday evening, Dec. 27th, under the same plan as last year.

 

The ladies' society of St. Peter's church will meet at the residence of the rector on Friday afternoon and evening of next week.

 

C. H. Butler has a very attractive assortment of Christmas and New Year’s cards which he is selling as cheap as the cheapest.

 

In the absence of Rev. Mr. Bailey, Mr. Bailey, Jr., will supply at Christ's church next Sunday morning and St. Peter's in the afternoon. There will be no service in the latter in the forenoon, but the Sunday school will convene at the usual hour.

 

 

SOUTHFORD.

 

Hurrah for Christmas!

 

The rain following the snow has left the roads icy and slippery.

 

Davis' stage now goes on runners.

 

Samuel L. Tuttle is teaching the Hull's Hill school.

 

Herbert Richards is putting a quietus on porcine quadrupeds.

 

The snow last Sunday made good sleighing.

 

 

QUAKER FARMS.

 

There was no service in the church on Sunday las* «»n account of the storm.

 

The family of C. A. Meigs has moved to Waterbury for better school advantages for their children. We hope the move will not be permanent, for they will be missed here.

 

Mrs. Ellen Tomlinson and daughter hare gone to Sandy Hook for the winter.

 

Mr. Roswell Hinman, an old resident of this place, was buried on Friday last in the old cemetery, Rev. Mr. Snow officiating.

 

Frank Wooster met with a serious accident in cutting his foot with an ax and nearly severing his toes, while cutting timber for E. D. Tomlinson.

 

We are gathering material for a much needed cemetery fence. It was the intention to build it this fall, but Jack Frost has put in a remonstrance and we shall be compelled to postpone the work until spring.

 

 

SQUANTUCK.

 

P. and E. Tomlinson are rushing business at their mill.

 

Miss Grace Tomlinson is visiting with her aunt in Ansonia.

 

Henry Wheeler has moved into the house of Charles Gilbert.

 

Felix Wood is occupying the new house built by Mr. Hanchett.

 

A new enterprise has started on Rockhouse Hill. Stanley Vietz and E. J. Davis are tanning sheep-skins with the wool on, and manufacturing robes, mats, etc.

 

Joseph Peet has moved to Rockhouse Hill, into the house of the late Sarah Chatfield.

 

Mrs. Clemons has sold the black pony, and Mr. Clemons has been presented with a fine horse by the Birmingham Iron Foundry.

 

A Mr. French has hired the old Eben Bassett place.

 

Stephen Mansfield has been visiting at F. M. Clemons' a few days. He is not very well this winter, being afflicted with a bad cough.
