Seymour Record, August 11, 1882

 

Oxford.

F. 0. Cable and son have returned to their home in Owego, N. Y.

The Congregational sociable will take place as usual on Thursday of next week.

There will be a meeting of the Oxford Agricultural Society on Thursday, Aug. 17th, at 2 p. m., at the residence of Wooster B. McEwen.

All of the wires of the Mutual Union Telegraph Co., were obstructed west for two days after the shower which passed South-west on Monday.

Rev. Mr. Bailey has gone on a three weeks vacation to his home in Maine. Mr. John H. McCrackan a recent grad­uate of Trinity college will supply in St. Peter's church next and the follow­ing Sunday and afternoon.

Lewis B. Hubbell lost his bay horse in a very unusual manner on Thursday morning. Mr. H. went out to catch him to drive to his work, and in his attempt to jump through the bars the animal tumbled, striking partly on his head and in all probability burst an artery as he died in about half an hour. One leg is also supposed to have been broken.

There was a large attendance at the picnic of the two societies, and includ­ing Quaker Farms, at Quassapaug last Thursday and a very pleasant time is reported. Nothing to mar the occasion, excepting, perhaps, some got a little damp by a shower corning suddenly over. But even this ought to have been a pleasure to those who had seen no rain for four or five weeks. It certainly would have been very acceptable to some who remained home as the shower hit the town of Oxford only slightly.
