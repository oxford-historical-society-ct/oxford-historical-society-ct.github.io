1883_09-21_Seymour-Record

 

EARLY HISTORY OF THE Oxford Congregational Church.

 

9.

 

Att an adjurned society meetting in the Parrish of oxford and held att the House of mr John Twichels, cenyour, on the seventh Day of march, 17412/3

 

Voted and past in said meetting that the meetting House that was agreed upon to be built, and pased by a major vote att a Lawful Society meetting on ye 20th Day of October, 1741, att the house of mr Sam11 Twichels in sd parrish, and said meetting House being sett up upon the place that the General assembly's Committee has affixed, and that said meetting House shall be Devoted to the Presbyterian ministry for Ever,

 

Voted and past in said meetting that ye Fourty Shillings that mr Timothy Johnson gave to this society shall go to Isaac Trowbridge towards what the said society is indebted to said Trowbridge.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that theare shall be a Rate Leveyed of 12 pence upon the pound on the List on ye year 1742, and that said Rate shall be for the building of ye above sd meetting and to be collected and gathered by the laft of may next.

 

Voted and past in said meeting that Moses Bristol shall be a collector to collect and gather the said 12 peney Rate for the meetting House acording to the above said time prefixed.

 

this present meetting is ajurned by a major Vote to the thirty-first Day of this instant, march, att Eight of the aclock in the morning, att the Houfe of mr John Twichels, Cenyour.

 

Test pr me, ISAAC TROWBRIDGE,

Society Clerk.

 

 

10.

 

Att a Society meetting held by an ajurnment att the Honfe of mr John Twichels, cenyour, on ye thirty-first Day of march, 1743.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that the said Laft 12 peney Rate that was granted for the building of the meetting House was to be in the old Tenor.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that mr Isaac Knowles and Isaac Trowbridge shall be auditors to Recken and A Just all acounts with all the collectors in said Parrish that then was.

 

Voted and past in said meetting to go to the assembly in may next att Hartford to obtain the Liberty of settling a church acording to the Presbiterian constitution.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that Isaac Trowbridge shall be our agent to go the assembly in may next att Hartford to Represent the society of the Parrish of oxford to pray for a Tax upon all the unimproved Land that is layed out and of obtaining the Liberty of gathering a church in said Parrish acording to the Presbyterian constitution.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that mr Ebenezar Riggs and mr Joseph Davis and mr James Wheler be a committee to Lay out a pice of Land for a burying place in said Whelers Land by the meetting House.

 

Test pr me ISAAC TROWBRIDGE,

Society Clerk.

 

 

OXFORD

 

The morning service will be omitted in St. Peters church next Sunday.

 

The Advertiser is out this week enlarged to 15x5 1/2, success to Johnny.

 

The town reports will probably be ready for distribution next Monday.

 

Miss Ella M. Lines commenced teaching a school in Middlebury last Monday.

 

C. B. Perry sailed on the steamer Knickerbocker for New Orleans, last Saturday.

 

Fred A. Bailey, son of Rev. Mr. Bailey, has been engaged to teach the centre school, which will commence Oct 1st.

 

The infant son of E. B. and Sarah A. Treat was baptized in St. Peter's church last Sunday, Rev. Mr. Bailey administering the rite.

 

The democrats of this town will nominate candidates for town officers at the town hall on Friday evening of next week, Sept. 28th.

 

The Guild Glee Club gave a serenade in front of the post-office and several residences last Saturday evening. The next meeting of the guild will be held at the residence of C. H. Butler on Saturday evening of this week and a general attendance is invited.

 

The annual meeting of the 3d or Chestnut-tree Hill School district was held last Friday evening. E. Wales Chatfield was elected committee and treasurer, and E. B. Treat clerk. Atwater Treat, who has been trustee of the Clark Fund for 32 years, was reelected for the 33d term. This fund was given to the district by Sheldon Clark in 1840. A sketch of his life, with portrait, may be found in the History of Seymour, pages 227-9. [EDITOR’S NOTE: This book is available online at https://archive.org/details/historyofseymour00shar_0 ] The last winter term of the school continued 16 weeks, and the summer term 14 weeks. The average attendance for the winter term was 12 1/3, for the summer term, 12. The whole number registered was 20.

 

The old records of the district are a curiosity, in the rates of wages recorded as paid to teachers. On the 16th of March, 1849, this district voted to employ a teacher for six months at $2 per week and board herself, and about this time Miss Nancy Humphreys taught for $1.25 per week and boarded herself. An old resident offers this explanation of these low prices of thirty odd years ago, that the young ladies boarded at home and taught simply because they liked the occupation and for a little dress money.
