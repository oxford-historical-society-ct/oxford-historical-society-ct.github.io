1883_12-14_Seymour-Record

 

 

EARLY HISTORY OF THE Oxford Congregational Church.

(These reprints follow the original record in spelling and capitalization.) The records copied below are omitted, being the same as those last published—Nov. 23d.

 

 

Art a Society meetting held by ajurnment on ye first monday of January, 1746, att the meetting House in ye Parish of oxford, John Chatfield chosen clerk for said day.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that the Society Committee shall Reckon with and make up accounts with ye Collector of the Land Tax.

 

Said meetting ajurned by a major vote To ye first monday of february next att 3 of ye Clock in ye afternoon att the meetting House in ye Parish of oxford.

 

Att a society meetting held by ajurnment on ye first monday of february, 1745-6, att ye meetting House in the Parish of oxford.

 

Voted and past in said meetting to Levy a Rate of thirteen pence upon ye pound on the List of year 1745, for to pay the first years sallery to mr Lyman.

 

Voted and past in said meetting to Levy a Rate of thirteen pence upon the ground on the List of the year 1745 to pay ye first year's settelment to mr Lyman.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that Sart Isaac Knowles shall be Collector to gather the above said sallery & settelment Rates by the Last of October next.

 

Voted and past in said meeting that the said meetting House Committee shall Receive the second year's Land Tax and to Lay it out according to the ends it was granted for.

 

Att a Society meetting Lawfully warned and held att the meetting House in the Parish of oxford on the fifth Day of January, 1746-7, Isaac Trowbridge by a major vote was chosen Society Clerk.

 

Att said meetting Capt Timothy Russel was chosen modderator for sd meetting.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that Capt Timothy T. Russel and Capt John Lumm and Ensign John Chatfield shall be the Society Committee for this present year.

 

Voted and past in said meetting to give Sarjant James Wheeler fifteen pounds Old Tenor for getting mr Lyman his fire wood untill the fifth Day of January next Ensuing.

 

Said meeting is ajurned to the Second Day of march next att 12 of the Clock att the meetting House in ye Parish of oxford.

 

Att a. Society meetting held by an ajurnment in ye Parish of oxford, att the meetting House on the Second Day of March, 1746-7.

 

Said meetting is ajurned by a major vote to the twenty sixth Day of october next att twelve of the Clock in said Day att the meetting House in the Parish of oxford.

 

Att a Society meetting held by an ajurnment att the meetting House in the Parish of oxford on the twenty sixth Day of October, 1747.

 

Voted and past in Said meetting to Levy a Rate in ye old Tenor of fifteen pence upon the pound upon the List of the year 1746 to pay mr Lyman his Second year's Sallery with the Discount of the money and fifteen pence upon the pound in ye old Tenor upon the List of the year 1746 to pay mr Lyman his Second year's settelment with the Discount of the money.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that Joseph Osborn shall be a Collector to gather the said two fifteen peny Rates.

 

 

OXFORD.

 

John Crowther of Seymour, moved into the Walker house in Red City on Tuesday, and vacated the same on the following day.

 

David Roberts' family have moved from Southford to their place in Red City which was recently vacated by D. C. Dorman.

 

What Christmas decorating is done in St. Peter's church will have to be done next week and therefore the need of all help possible.

 

There is no doubt but that all who attend the Guild entertainment on Saturday evening will get the worth of their money.  Admission 15 and 10 cts.

 

A select assortment of cards, books, and other articles suitable for Christmas presents can be found at the post office, at prices as cheap as the cheapest.

 

The annual meeting of the Congregational Society will be held in the vestibule on Monday next at 10 a. m.

 

The children's Christmas tree entertainment of St Peter's church will be held on Christmas eve., Monday, the 24th, the presents to be furnished by parents and friends, and it is hoped that the tree will be generally patronized. Contributions to purchase confectionery and oranges can be left with E. B. Treat and C H. Butler.

 

A large number assembled to pay the last tribute of respect to their friend and neighbor, John Harger, whose funeral was attended from his late residence on Five Mile Hill Monday afternoon, Rev. Mr. Root of Seymour officiating. The death of Mr. Harger makes a vacancy in this and adjoining towns which seems not easy to till. The post mortem indicated that the inward difficulty was such that no medical skill could have availed to have cured him. Mr. H. has been a member of the board of education a number of years and was re-elected last fall, when he was also re-elected town auditor.

 

 

SOUTHFORD.

 

After spending a few months in Europe, Doct. E. Oatman returned home in good health to gladden the hearts of his very many friends. May success attend the young doctor in his chosen vocation.

 

 

HULL'S HILL.

 

We seldom get in print, yet we are a stirring people. During the past week the principal business has been building. D. Johnson with the assistance of Mr. Northrop of Huntington has put up quite a hennery. John Roswell has employed a carpenter from New Haven to put an addition on his hennery, so we may expect poultry to be plenty, if some of the hunters will but kill the foxes that are so troublesome.

 

Granville Warner is building a barn to put that new trotter in. D. Gary is doing the joiner work.

 

Martin Stowe is moving into the place lately vacated by Mrs. Johnson.

 

There was a molasses candy party at Warren Stowe's Monday evening.
