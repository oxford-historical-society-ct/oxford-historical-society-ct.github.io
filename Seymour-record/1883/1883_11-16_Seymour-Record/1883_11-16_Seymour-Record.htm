1883_11-16_Seymour-Record

 

SOUTHFORD AND SEYMOUR MAIL WAGON.

 

Will leave Southford post-office at 7:30; Oxford at 8.10; arriving at Seymour in time for the 9 o'clock train. Will leave Seymour on arrival of the 10.36 train, arriving in Southford in time for either the east or west hound train on the N. Y. & N. E. R. R. All orders left at either of the above named post offices will he promptly attended to.   Fares: From Southford to Seymour, 25c; Oxford to Seymour, 15c.

H. T. EDWARDS.

 

 

OXFORD.

 

The Bishop of the diocese is expected to visit St. Peter's church on Sunday. Nov. 25th.

 

The guild meet this week Saturday evening at the residence of F. A. Keeney, Southford.

 

All who are interested in the much needed improvements in and around the Episcopal cemetery are invited to meet at the residence of C. W. Butler on Saturday evening of this week.

 

George Smith, who lived at the Marvin Sanford place in Quaker Farms and who was 77 years of age, died suddenly while on a visit last Monday in Bethlehem. Interment at Quaker Farms on Wednesday, the 14th.

 

 

CRYSTAL WEDDING.

 

The most enjoyable party of the season thus far occurred on Monday evening, Nov. 5th, the 15th anniversary of the marriage of Mr. and Mrs. C. H. Butler. The evening was fine and the ride hence was really enjoyable.

 

Arriving at our destination, we soon found that the brightness outside was excelled by that within, while the genial faces and cherry words which welcomed us to the charmed circle made us feel at once the joys of friendship. Some two hundred of the friends and relatives of the happy couple assembled to offer congratulations and to wish them long life and continued prosperty.

 

Tonight we meet to celebrate

With happy hearts and gay—

The fifteenth anniversary,

Of this, your wedding day.

 

Full fifteen years have come and gone

Since you two, hand in hand,

The path of life together trod,

The happiest in the land.

 

But as we gather here to-night,

Where yon have dwelt so long—

We'd speak our heartfelt wishes forth

In words of gladsome song.

 

We'd wish for you in future days

Much more of smiles than tears,

That God would bless and spare you both,

For many happy years.

 

Many beautiful and useful presents were brought as token of friendship and esteem, including a two lamp extension chandelier from the Oxford Guild, a choice tea set of decorated china, containing 56 pieces, an elegant bedroom set of new design, also a general line of crystal ware including one dozen tinted berry dishes, cake stand, a preserve dish presented by Mr. and Mrs. Lewis Barnes, M. D, a preserve dish by Mr. and Mrs. Hedges of Birmingham, a pickle jar, silver mounted, by N. J. Warner; a druggist glass by Birmingham friends; a pitcher representing an owl by Mr. and Mrs. Wm. Butler, and butter dishes, pickle jars, celery holders, and numerous other useful gifts from friends and neighbors.

 

Miss Anna L. Butler received a handsome shell purse and crystal tea set.

 

The presentation speech was made by John H. McCracken, and happily acknowledged with thanks by Mr. Butler in a few well chosen remarks.

 

Rev. W. C. Roberts and others made responses, after which a bountiful collation was served to all present. The Guild entertained the company with music at the house, and Messrs. Stuart, Leek and Robinson furnished music for those who cared to dance at the Town Hall, after which the party dispersed wishing the bride and groom many happy returns of their wedding anniversary.

 

 

SOUTHFORD.

 

Celery and other grain crops will soon be thrashed.

 

Apples, turnips and overcoats have been picked and housed.

 

H. T. Edwards, our accommodating mail carrier, is always on time.

 

Davis' stage runs on time daily and all errands promptly attended to.

 

Trains run regularly from this city to the villages of Waterbury and Danbury, and business is brisk.

 

Jack Frost is laying siege to this village and though repelled in the midday sorties, led by old Sol, he more than regains the lost ground before the next dawn.

 

Mrs. W. F. Bassett, from Washington Territory, arrived yesterday on a visit to her mother and her brother, Arthur J. Lewis. She will remain during the winter. Her husband's farm is near two of the newly discoveied silver mines,about which there has been great excitement.

 

 

 

EARLY HISTORY OF THE Oxford Congregational Church.

 

30.

Att a society meetting held by ajurnment on the 21 day august, 1745, in ye parish of oxford att the meeting house

 

Said meeting ajurned by a major vote to the 18 day of September next at one of the clock in ye afternoon at the meeting house in the Parish of oxford

Pest pr me,

ISAAC TROWBRIDGE,

Society Clerk

 

31.

Att a society meetting held by ajurnment on ye 18th day of September, 1745 at ye meetting House in ye Parish of Oxford

 

Voted and past in said meetting To Keep up the currantcy in ye proposals made to the Worthy mr Jonathan Lyman both as to Settelment and Sallery as good as it is when said Society enters into covenant with ye said mr Jonathan Lyman.

 

Voted and past in said meeting to attend upon the ordination of ye Worthy mr. Jonathan Lyman upon ye fourth Wenesday of October next.

 

Voted and past in said meeetting to Keep a day of fast in order for settelment on ye first Wensday of October next.

 

Said meeting is ajurned by a major vote to ye first Monday in October next att 8 of ye clock in ye morning att ye meeting House in ye Parish of oxford.

 

Test pr me

ISAAC TROWBRIDGE,

Society Clerk

 

32.

Att a Society meetting held by ajurnment on ye first Monday of October 1743, in ye Parrish of oxford att ye meetting House.

 

Voted and past in said meetting To give the Worthy mr Jonathan Lyman his fire wood during ye time that the worthy mr Lyman continues with us in the work of the ministry.

 

Voted and past in said meetting to attend on ye ordination of ye Worthy mr Jonathan Lyman on the 30 dat of this instant October.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that Capt. Timothy Russell, Capt John Lumm and Joseph Tomlinson to provide for ye entertainbment of ye ministers and messengers at ye ordination.

 

Voted and past in said meeting to keep a day of fast on ye 16 day of this instant October in order for settelment.

 

Said meeting is ajurned by a major vote to ye first Monday in November next att one of ye clock in ye afternoon att ye meetting House in ye Parish of oxford.

 

Test pr me,

ISAAC TROWBRIDGE

Society Clerk.

 

 

33.

Att a society meeting held by ajurnment on ye first Monday of November, 1745, att the meeting House in the Parish of oxford.

 

Voted and past in sd meetting to grant a Rate of fifteen pence upon ye pound upon ye list of ye year 1744 to Defray ye ministerial charges of said society.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that sarjant Isaac Knowles shall be a collector to gather said fifteen peny Rate.

 

Said meeting is ajurned by a major vote to ye first Monday of Decmber next att eight of ye Clock in ye morning att ye meetting House in the Parish of oxford.

 

Test pr me,

ISAAC TROWBRIDGE,

Society Clerk.

 

 

34.

Att a society meetting Lawfully warned and held at ye meetting House in the Parish of oxford on the 6th day of December, 1745, Capt Timothy Russell by a major vote was chosen moderator for said meetting.

 

Att said meeting Isaac Trowbridge by a major vote was chosen Society Clerk.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that Capt. Timothy Russell and Capt John Lumm and Ensign John Chatfield shall be a Society's Comt'ee for the year insuing.

 

Said meeting is ajurned by a major vote to the first Monday of January next insuing att 3 of clock in the afternoon att the meeting House in the Parish of oxford.

 

Test pr me,

ISAAC TROWBRIDGE,

Society Clerk
