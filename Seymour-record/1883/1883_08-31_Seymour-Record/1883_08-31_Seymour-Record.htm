Oxford Congregational Church.  No. 2.

 

October ye 6th day, 1741, att a society meetting Lawfully warned and held in the Parrish of oxford att the house of mr John Twichel. mr John Chatfield was chosen by a major vote modderator for said meetting.

 

Voted and Past by two thirds parts of the inhabitants of said Parrish or society, who by Law are qualifyed to vote, and present in said meetting, to build a meetting house, and to move to the assembly in their next session att new haven, to pray for a Committee to appoint, order and affix ye Place wheare on their meetting House shall be Erected and built.

 

Voted and past in said meeting that mr Ebenezar Riggs shall be our agent to the assembly, to manage ye affairs of obtaining a Committee for ye above said business.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that mr Caleb Perry, mr Ebenezar Riggs, mr John Lumm,|mr John Towner, mr Samul Woster, Shall be a committee to Sign bills.

 

Voted and past in said meetting to Levy a Rate of onepeney upon ye pound on the List in ye year 1740, in order to Defray the Charges of a Court Committee, &c.

 

Voted and past in said meeting that mr Roger Purkins shall be collector and treasurer, and to gather said Rate by ye 15th day of this instant month.

 

Voted and past in said meetting to Disanul that vote that was past to hier a minister upon probation.

 

Voted and past in said meeting to hier a minister for this present year.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that Sarjeant Timothy Woster and mr James Wheller and mr Eliphelett Bristol to be a committee to hier a minister for this present year.

 

Voted and past in said meeting that the methord to warn our society meettings shall be by advertisements.

 

Voted and past in said meeting that the places to set up the advertisements to warn ye society society meetings shall be mr John Lum's house, mr Jonathan Griffin's house, mr Joseph Lewis, his house, mr Isaac Knowles, his house, mr Joseph Davis, his house and mr Joseph Wood's house.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that mr Samll Twichel's house shall be ye place to meet on the Sabath till ye year is ended.

 

Test pr me, ISAAC TROWBRIDGE,

Society Clerk.

 

 

October ye 20th day, 1741, att a society meetting Lawfully warned and held in the Parrish of oxford, at the house of mr Samll Twichel. mr Jonathan Griffin by a major vote was chosen modderator for said meeting.

 

Voted and past in said meetting to build a meetting House thirty Eight foot Long and thirty two foot wide and nineteen foot posts. Voted and past in said meetting that mr John Chatfield & mr Roger Purkins and mr Samuel Twichel shall be a committee with full power to manage all ye affairs of building ye said meeting House for said Parrish acord-ing to the above sd vote.

 

Voted and past in said meetting to Levy a Rate of 12 pence upon ye pound upon the List in ye year 1741, in order to ye carring on of ye building of said meeting house, and that said Rate shall be collected and gathered by the first of march next insuing,

 

Voted and past in said meetting that mr John Twichel, Junr, shall be a collector to collect and gather the said Rate.

 

Test pr me, ISAAC TROWBRIDGE,

Society Clerk.

 

 

December ye 9th day 1741, att a society meetting Lawfully warned and held in the Parrish of oxford, att ye house of mr Samll Twichel. mr Isaac Knowles by a major vote was chosen modderator for said meetting.

 

Att said meetting Isaac Trowbridge by a major vote was chosen clerk for said society.

 

At said meetting, voted & past that mr John Chatfield and mr Samll Woster, mr La Chari Hawkins to be a committee for said sociey and to sign bills.

 

Voted and past in sd meetting that mr John Lum and mr Roger Pitkins and Eliphelett Bristol shall be a committee with full power to hier a minister upon probation.

 

Voted and past in said meetting to give mr Buckinham a call upon probation.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that said minister committee shall have full power if mr Buckinham cannot be obtained to go till they can obtain one,

 

Voted and past in said meetting that mr Ebenezar Riggs and mr John Lum and mr James Wheller shall be agents for the Parrish of oxford, to go to the town of Derby to request of said town a settlement for ye ministry & to provid highways.

 

Voted & past in said meetting to Levy a rate of three pence upon ye pound on the List in the year 1741, in order to Defray the charges of ye society.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that mr Sam11 Wheller shall be collector & treasurer, and that said Rate shall be collected by the first day of January next insuing.

 

Voted and past in said meetting that mr Samll Twichel's House shall be the Place to meett att on the Sabath Days, warning by notifications Renewed by said society's vote att said meetting.

 

Test pr me, ISAAC TROWBRIDGE.

Society Clerk.

 

October ye 20th day, 1741, att a society meetting Lawfully warned and held in the Parrish of oxford, at the house of mr Samll Twichel. mr Jonathan Griffin by a major vote was chosen modderator for said meeting.

 

Voted and past in said meetting to build a meetting House thirty Eight foot Long and thirty two foot wide and nineteen foot posts. Voted and past in said meetting that mr John Chattield & mr Roger Purkins and mr Samuel Twichel shall be a committee with full power to manage all ye affairs of building ye said meeting House for said Parrish acording to the above sd vote.

 

Voted and past in said meetting to Levy a Rate of 12 pence upon ye pound upon the List in ye year 1741, in order to ye carring on of ye building of said meeting house, and that said Rate shall be collected and gathered by the first of march next insuing,

 

Voted and past in said meetting that mr John Twichel, Junr, shall be a collector to collect and gather the said Rate.

 

Test pr me, ISAAC TROWBRIDGE,

Society Clerk.

 

 

December ye 9th day 1741, att a society meetting Lawfully warned and held in the Parrish of oxford, att ye house of mr Samll Twichel. mr Isaac Knowles by a major vote was chosen modderator for said meetting.

 

Att said meetting Isaac Trowbridge by a major vote was chosen clerk for said society.

 

 

 

SQUANTUCK.

 

E. J. Davis sawed his thumb badly with a hand saw.

 

Samuel Nason and family moved away from this place on Monday morning.

 

Mrs. Clemons is slowly convalescing, but is able to use her arm but a little.

 

Mrs. A. J. Downs scalded her arm and hand by spilling a kettle of hot water.

 

Mr. Tolles has lost all his garden vegetables by cows that are pastured on the highway.

 

W. F. Gillette and family from New Haven are visiting at his father's, on Great Hill.

 

Eli Gillett is having quite an extensive hennery built. E. W. Clark is doing the carpenter work.

 

The river road is in a terrible condition through the town of Derby, and it also needs repairing above the Squantuck bridge.

 

Those young men that were in bathing last Sunday between Clemons' and Tomlinson's will be liable to arrest for indecent exposure if they repeat it.

Rev. Mr. and Mrs. Snow took their departure on Thursday for their new home in South Windsor.

 

There were about 150 in attendance at the Congregational church last Sunday morning, when Rev. Mr. Snow closed his services here.

 

The Congregational social circle was largely attended on Wednesday and was made the occasion of a farewell to Rev. Mr. Snow, at whose going there is a general regret.

 

Rector Bailey returned last Saturday evening apparently very much improved in health. Next Sunday he will be at St. Peter's church all day and will celebrate the holy communion in the morning.

 

The Democrats of this town seem to have taken the lead of the county in calling a caucus for the election of delegates to the conventions yet to be called. They will meet at the town hall on Saturday evening of this week, to choose delegates to the county and senatorial conventions.

 

 

SOUTHFORD.

 

A peach festival will be given at Southford chapel Tuesday evening, Sept. 4th. Peaches and cream and ice cream will be served, and a good time may be expected. The proceeds will go towards completing the furnishing of the chapel.
